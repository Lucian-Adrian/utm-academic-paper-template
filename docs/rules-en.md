# UTM Writing Rules and Formats for Abstracts, Papers, and Thesis/Projects

This document consolidates requirements from:

- `Abstract_template-Conf-UTM-2026-rom.docx`;
- `Paper_template-Conf-SMD-UTM-2026-rom.docx`;
- `sustinereTezeLicenta.pdf`;
- the older folders `UTM Latex Template` and `Thesis_Report_Template_EN`.

Its purpose is to provide a practical reference for writing, checking, and recreating a UTM academic paper in LaTeX. For academic articles/papers, the primary source is `Paper_template-Conf-SMD-UTM-2026-rom.docx`; thesis/project rules apply only to license thesis/project documents or optional title pages requested separately.

## Priority Rule

Choose the document type before writing:

- conference abstract: use `Abstract_template-Conf-UTM-2026-rom.docx`;
- conference paper/article: use `Paper_template-Conf-SMD-UTM-2026-rom.docx`;
- license thesis/project: use `sustinereTezeLicenta.pdf`;
- stricter teacher, supervisor, department, or committee instruction: follow the stricter instruction and record the deviation from the template.

Do not mix the 12,000-character conference-paper limit with the 60-80-page thesis limit.

## Teacher's Enumeration Rule

For enumerations:

- the introductory sentence ends with a colon;
- each item starts with a lowercase letter;
- each item ends with a semicolon;
- the last item also keeps the semicolon if the teacher requires uniformity;
- this rule does not apply to numbered bibliography entries, which follow the reference style.

Correct example:

```text
the main contributions of the paper are:
a) development of the analysis model;
b) validation of the obtained results;
c) formulation of final recommendations;
```

## General Academic Rules

- the work must be original;
- every source used for ideas, text, figures, tables, schemes, formulas, scanned images, or adapted material must be cited;
- plagiarism includes reusing figures, tables, and schemes without citation;
- the author is responsible for the accuracy and truthfulness of the results;
- the title must be short, precise, free of non-standard acronyms, and understandable to non-specialists;
- abbreviations must be explained at first use;
- symbols and notations must be explained at first use unless a notation list is provided;
- use an impersonal academic style and avoid first person;
- language must be clear, direct, logical, and coherent;
- Romanian text must use diacritics;
- avoid vague wording, ambiguity, and unsupported claims.

## Common Conference Template Page Setup

For both conference abstract and paper:

- paper size: A4;
- orientation: portrait;
- top margin: 2.0 cm;
- bottom margin: 2.0 cm;
- right margin: 2.0 cm;
- left margin: 2.5 cm;
- header distance: 1.0 cm;
- footer distance: 1.25 cm;
- main font: Times New Roman;
- main body text: 12 pt;
- body alignment: justified.

## Conference Abstract

Required order:

1. paper title;
2. authors;
3. affiliations;
4. corresponding author;
5. scientific supervisor/coordinator;
6. abstract;
7. keywords.

Title:

- uppercase;
- centered;
- Times New Roman, 14 pt;
- bold;
- single line spacing.

Authors:

- format: `First name LAST NAME`;
- centered;
- Times New Roman, 12 pt;
- bold;
- each author has a numeric affiliation marker;
- the corresponding author is marked with `*`.

Affiliations:

- include department, group, faculty, university, city, country;
- centered;
- 10 pt;
- italic;
- each affiliation starts with the matching numeric marker.

Corresponding author:

- begins with `*Autorul corespondent:`;
- includes first name, last name, and email;
- centered;
- 10 pt;
- normal style.

Scientific supervisor/coordinator:

- includes name, scientific title, and affiliation;
- centered;
- 10 pt;
- label and name are bold in the template.

Abstract:

- begins with `Rezumat.`;
- label is bold italic;
- body is italic;
- Times New Roman, 12 pt;
- justified;
- approximately half a page;
- includes research purpose, methodology, main results, and relevant conclusions;
- emphasizes the novelty of the work;
- avoids general statements, ambiguity, and unexplained abbreviations.

Keywords:

- begin with `Cuvinte cheie:`;
- label is bold italic;
- body is italic;
- Times New Roman, 12 pt;
- justified;
- 4 to 6 keywords;
- lowercase;
- comma-separated;
- avoid repeating title terms.

## Conference Paper / Academic Article

Normal structure:

1. title;
2. authors;
3. affiliations;
4. corresponding author;
5. scientific supervisor/coordinator;
6. abstract;
7. keywords;
8. introduction and problem statement;
9. experimental or methodological part;
10. results and discussion;
11. conclusions;
12. acknowledgements, if applicable;
13. references;
14. originality and responsibility declaration.

Limits:

- maximum 12,000 characters including spaces;
- abstract length: 150-200 words;
- no references in the abstract.

Body text:

- Times New Roman;
- 12 pt;
- justified;
- new paragraphs must be visible;
- use automatic page numbering.

Sections:

- template section headings are Times New Roman, 12 pt, bold;
- use short and structurally parallel titles;
- typical sections include `Introducere`, methodology/experimental part, `Rezultate si discutii`, `Concluzii`, `Multumiri`, and `Referinte`.

Scientific expectations:

- original papers and short communications must contain original findings;
- enough detail must be provided to reproduce experiments;
- review articles must provide new information and synthesis, not only summaries of previous work.

Tables:

- centered;
- full page width when possible;
- if continued on the next page, mark the continuation and repeat headers;
- table text: 10 pt, single line spacing;
- column headers are explanatory, bold, and include units where applicable;
- numbered by order of appearance: `Tabelul 1`, `Tabelul 2`;
- cited in text as `Tab. 1`, `Tab. 2`;
- title appears above the table;
- table title is 11 pt and bold.

Figures:

- centered;
- placed as close as possible to the first text mention;
- numbered by order of appearance: `Figura 1`, `Figura 2`;
- cited in text as `Fig. 1`, `Fig. 2`;
- have a short and accurate caption;
- caption begins with bold `Figura` and the number;
- caption is centered;
- caption is 11 pt;
- images must have good resolution without being disproportionately large.

Equations and formulas:

- use Equation Editor, MathType, or correct LaTeX math;
- equations are centered;
- equations are numbered consecutively with Arabic numerals in round parentheses;
- numbering is placed at the right margin;
- cite in text as `Ec. (1)`, `Ec. (2)`.

Symbols, notations, and units:

- symbols are italic, 12 pt;
- vectors are bold;
- standard functions such as `log`, `ln`, and `exp` are normal/upright;
- use internationally accepted symbols;
- SI units are recommended;
- use uppercase `L` for liter;
- use lowercase `k` for kilogram, not uppercase `K`;
- decimal numbers use a point, not a comma;
- large formulas and schemes should be presented as figures.

References:

- use Arabic numerals in square brackets in text;
- number references in citation order;
- place citation before punctuation;
- accepted forms: `[1]`, `[1-4]`, `[1,3]`;
- every cited reference must appear in the list and every listed reference must be cited;
- full journal names must be written, not abbreviated;
- reference list uses Times New Roman, 12 pt;
- Zotero, EndNote, Mendeley, or ReferenceManager are recommended.

Reference models:

```text
1. AUTHOR 1, A.B.; AUTHOR 2, C.D. Paper title. Journal Name Year, Volume, pp. 154-196, doi:
2. AUTHOR 1, A.; AUTHOR 2, B. Book title, 3rd ed.; Publisher: Publisher location, Country, Year; pp. 154-196.
3. AUTHOR 1, A.B. Thesis title. Thesis level, University, Location, Defense date.
4. Website title. Available online: URL (accessed on Day Month Year).
```

Declaration:

- included at the end of the paper template;
- the corresponding author declares originality;
- proper citation of all bibliographic sources is confirmed;
- include full name, signature, and date.

## License Thesis / Project

Length and proportions:

- explanatory memorandum: 60-80 pages excluding annexes;
- theoretical part: about 30-40% and not more than 40%;
- practical/applied part: about 60-70%;
- figures and graphs must not exceed 30% of total volume.

Structure:

1. cover;
2. title page;
3. assignment/task sheet;
4. dedication and acknowledgements page, optional;
5. student declaration;
6. supervisor opinion;
7. abstract;
8. table of contents;
9. list of tables, optional;
10. list of figures/graphs/diagrams/schemes, optional;
11. list of abbreviations, optional;
12. introduction;
13. main chapter content;
14. conclusions;
15. bibliography;
16. annexes, if applicable.

Formatting:

- accepted language: Romanian, English, French, or Russian;
- A4;
- print only on the front side of each sheet;
- Times New Roman, 12 pt;
- 1.5 line spacing;
- chapter and paragraph titles may be 13-14 pt and bold;
- the work is organized by chapters and paragraphs, except introduction and conclusions.

Thesis abstract:

- presents main ideas, results, and conclusions;
- includes counts of chapters, pages, figures, tables, and bibliographic sources;
- Romanian version: 300-500 words;
- English or French version translates the Romanian version, including the title.

Thesis bibliography:

- contains all sources used;
- has 15-30 titles;
- may include books, monographs, conference papers, articles, theses, reports, standards, norms, guides, web pages, and internal reports;
- citations may be numeric by citation order or alphabetical by first element;
- a page-specific citation may be written as `[8, p. 231]`;
- year is written with Arabic numerals;
- if there are more than three authors, later names may be omitted with `et. al.`.

Defense:

- presentation time: up to 10-15 minutes;
- present theme, purpose, tasks, solution variants, results, argumentation, technical-economic calculations, safety/protection measures, and conclusions;
- important claims must be supported by data, graphs, diagrams, tables, schemes, formulas, simulations, or software outputs;
- answers to questions should be short, clear, and direct;
- several preliminary defenses are recommended one week before the official defense.

## Recommended LaTeX for the Paper

The modern template created in this repository is:

- `utm-academic-paper-template/utmconf.cls`;
- `utm-academic-paper-template/main.tex`;
- `utm-academic-paper-template/tools/check_utm_article.py`;
- `utm-academic-paper-template/titlepage-demo.tex`, only for the optional UTM title page.

LaTeX rules:

- use `utmconf.cls` for the conference paper;
- UTM logo assets are included in `assets`, but logo display is disabled by default because the official paper template does not require a logo;
- if the teacher requests a logo/header, enable `\UTMShowLogotrue`;
- compile with Tectonic/XeTeX and Times New Roman for Romanian diacritics;
- write the abstract in the `rezumat` environment;
- write keywords with `\cuvintecheie{...}`;
- caption tables with `\utmtablecaption{...}`;
- caption figures with `\utmfigurecaption{...}`;
- content enumerations use `utmenumerate` and every `\item` ends with a semicolon.

Verification commands:

```powershell
cd "D:\uni\year2\guidelines\paper\utm-academic-paper-template"
python tools\check_utm_article.py
tectonic main.tex
tectonic titlepage-demo.tex
```

## Final Checklist

- correct document type selected;
- structure matches the document type;
- page format and margins are correct;
- font, size, and alignment are correct;
- abstract has the required length;
- keywords are 4-6, lowercase, and comma-separated;
- enumerations follow the teacher's rule;
- tables, figures, and equations are numbered and cited in text;
- references are cited in text and listed;
- no citation appears in the conference-paper abstract;
- all reused/adapted material is cited;
- Romanian text uses diacritics;
- declaration is included when required by the template.
